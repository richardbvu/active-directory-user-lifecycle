I ran into an RDP login issue while working on a domain-joined Windows 11 VM in my lab. When I tried to connect, I received an authorization error that made it seem like the account didn’t have permission to log in remotely.

I started by checking the basic prerequisites. I confirmed that Remote Desktop was enabled on the workstation and verified membership in the Remote Desktop Users group using the net localgroup "Remote Desktop Users" command.

Next, I reviewed the Group Policy configuration that controls RDP access. I checked the Allow log on through Remote Desktop Services policy and confirmed that Domain Admins were included. I also verified that the workstation was in the correct OU and that the GPO was properly linked.

To make sure policy was applying correctly, I ran gpupdate /force and then used gpresult /scope computer /r to confirm the settings were in effect.

At that point, everything looked correct from a configuration standpoint, so I reconsidered the connection method itself. I retried the RDP connection, but this time I manually entered the credentials instead of using the cached ones in the RDP client.

The connection worked immediately. That confirmed the issue wasn’t with permissions or Group Policy, but with stale cached credentials being used during authentication.
