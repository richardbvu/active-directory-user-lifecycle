# GPO Application Verification

## Purpose
Use this runbook when a Group Policy Object appears not to apply to a workstation or user.

## Scope
Windows domain-joined lab systems in `corp.test`.

## Common Symptoms
- Expected GPO settings do not appear on the workstation
- `gpresult /r` seems to show no applied GPOs
- A GPO is linked in GPMC, but the expected effect is missing

## Quick Rule
Always verify whether the GPO is a:
- **Computer Configuration** policy
- **User Configuration** policy

Do not assume a missing GPO under **User Settings** means the GPO failed.

## Troubleshooting Steps

### 1. Identify the policy type
Check the GPO in Group Policy Management and determine whether the setting is under:
- **Computer Configuration**
- **User Configuration**

### 2. Verify OU placement
Confirm the target object is in the correct OU:
- computer policies apply to the **computer object**
- user policies apply to the **user object**

### 3. Verify the GPO link
In Group Policy Management:
- confirm the GPO is linked to the correct OU
- confirm the link is enabled

### 4. Refresh policy
On the target workstation, run:

cmd
gpupdate /force
